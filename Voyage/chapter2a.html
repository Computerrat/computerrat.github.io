Chapter 2

6 months into the voyage...

"I just don't understand how an island could just disappear," you mutter to yourself, "accourding to the charts, there should be an island nearby." Once more, you pickup your spyglass and scan the horizon looking for any signs of land. The Captain approaches you looking very concerned.

"Our provisions are starting to run low. The men are counting on you to get us to the island so we can replinish our provisions."

You show the Captain the charts you've been studying.

"By my reckoning, that island should be right here, straight ahead. I just don't understand."

Suddenly, the Captain exclaims "Look! Over there! Do you see that?" You grab your spyglass and look where the Captain is pointing. You can just barely make out a speck on the horizon. "The wind is starting to pick up. I think we should go full speed ahead over to the island."

"But Captain, with all due respect, Sir, the island should not be over there, it should be straight ahead," you respond. The Captain gives you a long, hard look. "You are the navigator. You also know how desperate we are to find land. I'm trusting you to make the right decision!"
